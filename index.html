About Me

I was born and raised in Fort Myers, Florida. I grew up loving FSU college football and I went to the same highschool as my grandmother, Fort Myers High School. I grew up playing football and running track a majority of my childhood. My freshman year of high school, I was the 8th fastest in the nation for my age group in race I focused in, 400 m dash. I was on a path to be able to go to FSU on a track scholarship, that is until I dislocated my knee my junior year. It healed fine but I was noticelably slower because of it. I decided attended FGCU for civil engineering and interned at a civil engineering firm during my time there.

After a few years I realized long term, that wasn't for me. So I pursued a new venture where I started up a software development company with a friend of mine (whom I met in college). My focus in the company was project management, customer relations, and marketing. I focused on finding us clients and being the direct line of contact with them. As well, I would test the websites and report feedback to the developers. My partner focused more on managing the different devlopers and designers we worked with. He was the one who focused on the actual coding while I just looked at the final prodcut and test it from there.

Over a couple years, we built our portfolio up and worked with clients in multiple industries such as education, flight, fitness, dating apps etc. We even worked on a small project for Universal Studios to create the Facebook page for the M. Night Shyamalan movie Glass. However once Covid hit, that made communication with our development team difficult as most of them were located across the pond. At that time, I figured it would be a good idea start fresh and move up to Tallahassee since I wanted to move up there pretty much my whole childhood.

Since I moved up here, I mainly focused on working and building a little capital. I began working at a steak/beef processing plant as a maintanence technician just across the Georgia border. Over a short period of time, I got promoted to a production scheduler. The production scheduler uses software like excel to track and analyze data sets to be able to determine what raw materials to buy week to week; and what the plant needs to run day to day to be able to cover the customer's orders. The customer in this case being Walmart. If you are in the southeast region of the country, any steak that you see in a black tray, comes from that facility. I got to a point where I was saving the company comfortably 6 figures a week compared to person who was previously in that position. However it got to a point where I was consistently working 60+ hours, 6-7 days a week with no light at the end of the tunnel. So I resigned from my position with the idea in mind to transition back into software development.

Why Software Development?

With having experience in the industry in general, I have always found an interest in software devleopment. However, what really pulls me to it now more than ever is the fact that I have decided on a long term path I want to take, and software development is a stepping stone in that path. If I stick with this and take the time to become genuinely proficient in it, then it is a skill set I can always fall back on. As well, I have noticed over time that once I have hit a point where I can't learn anymore about a position, I get bored with it. With software development being an ever evolving industry there is always something new to learn. In the long run, I feel that the broad wealth of knowledge that both is and isn't yet established will never leave me with that feeling again.
